Main Types of Stars

Stars are characterized by their properties, such as color and temperature. Here are a few examples of the different types of stars.

  1. O-Type Stars - The hottest and bluest of all stars.
  2. White Dwarf - The aftermath of the death of a star the size of our sun or smaller.
  3. Neutron Stars - Very small and dense cores of exploded stars.
  4. Supergiants - Massive stars that are highly developed.
  5. Red Dwarf - Red and relatively cool.

How Do Stars Evolve?

Stars change over time, going through stages like protostar, main sequence, red giant, and eventually white dwarf or neutron star.
